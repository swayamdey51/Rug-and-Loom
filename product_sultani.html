A Carpet Born from the Himalayas

Nestled at an altitude of over 3,000 metres in the Chamoli District of Uttarakhand, where the air is thin and the winters are brutal, the Sultani Blue was conceived. The deep indigo tones of this carpet are not a designer's choice — they are the colours of the night sky over the Nanda Devi peaks, observed and absorbed by weavers who have lived beneath them for generations.

The geometric medallion at its centre is inspired by the rangoli patterns drawn at the doorsteps of Garhwali homes during Makar Sankranti — a festival of harvest and gratitude. Each border detail echoes the carved wooden lattice work found on the ancient temples of Gopeshwar.

This is not just a carpet. It is a portable piece of the Himalayan highlands — a fragment of a culture that has survived centuries of isolation, harsh climate, and modernisation, yet continues to produce some of the most refined textile art in the world.

1

Wool Selection

Only the finest clip of New Zealand Merino wool is used — soft, lustrous, and with a natural crimp that gives the carpet its characteristic sheen. The wool is hand-washed in Himalayan spring water before spinning.

2

Natural Dyeing

The yarn is dyed using plant-based and mineral dyes in copper vessels over wood fires. The indigo is fermented for 72 hours before the yarn is immersed. This process, unchanged for 400 years, produces colours that deepen beautifully with age.

3

Hand Knotting

The Persian Senneh knot is used — a single asymmetric knot that allows for finer detail and higher knot density. The weaver reads from a hand-drawn talim (grid chart) and ties each knot individually around the warp threads.

4

Washing & Finishing

Once off the loom, the carpet is washed in cold running water, stretched to exact dimensions, and sun-dried for three days. The pile is then hand-sheared to a uniform height using traditional hand scissors.

5

Quality Inspection

Every carpet undergoes a 47-point quality check by our master inspector. Knot density, pile uniformity, colour consistency, and structural integrity are all verified before the carpet is approved for sale.

6

Certificate of Authenticity

Each carpet comes with a handwritten certificate bearing the name of the lead weaver, the date of completion, the knot count, and the dye sources used. A QR code links to a digital provenance record.

The Weavers of Chamoli

The Sultani Blue was woven by the Rawat weaving cooperative in Gopeshwar, the administrative capital of Chamoli District, Uttarakhand. This cooperative was founded in 1987 by a group of 12 women who sought to preserve the dying art of Garhwali carpet weaving while creating sustainable livelihoods for mountain families.

Today, the cooperative employs 43 weavers — predominantly women between the ages of 22 and 60. Many are from households where weaving has been practised for four or five generations. The knowledge of pattern, knot, and dye is passed from mother to daughter, often without any written record.

Rug & Loom works exclusively with this cooperative under a fair trade agreement. Weavers receive 60% of the final sale price — significantly above the industry standard of 15-20%. We also fund the cooperative's healthcare programme and contribute to a scholarship fund for weavers' children.

Caring for Your Carpet

Vacuum Gently

Vacuum weekly in the direction of the pile using a low-suction setting. Never use a beater bar attachment — it damages the knots.

Rotate Every 6 Months

Rotate 180° every 6 months to ensure even wear and prevent one area from fading due to sunlight exposure.

Blot Spills Immediately

Blot (never rub) spills with a clean white cloth. For stubborn stains, use cold water and mild wool wash only.

Avoid Direct Sunlight

Prolonged direct sunlight will fade natural dyes. Use UV-filtering curtains if placing near a south-facing window.

Never Machine Wash

Machine washing will destroy the knot structure. Professional hand-washing every 3-5 years is recommended.

Professional Cleaning

We offer a complimentary professional cleaning service for all Rug & Loom carpets every 5 years. Simply contact us and we will arrange collection, cleaning at our Chamoli facility, and return delivery — all at no cost to you.

Storage Instructions

If storing, roll the carpet (never fold) pile-side inward around an acid-free tube. Wrap in breathable cotton muslin. Store in a cool, dry place. Never store in plastic — wool needs to breathe.
