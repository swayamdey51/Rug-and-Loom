Silence Woven into Silk

The Earth Silk Minimalist was born from a conversation between a master weaver and an architect — both from Chamoli, both seeking the same thing: a carpet that disappears into a room while making it feel complete. The result is a piece of rare restraint, where the beauty lies not in what is shown but in what is held back.

The palette — warm ecru, raw umber, and the faintest blush of burnt sienna — is derived entirely from the mineral-rich soils and river clays of the Pindar Valley, a tributary of the Alaknanda River that runs through the heart of Chamoli. These are the colours of the Himalayan earth at dawn, before the sun fully rises.

The silk used in this carpet is sourced from the mulberry silk farms of Uttarkashi — less than 200km from the weaving village — making this one of the few truly local luxury textiles produced entirely within a single mountain region.

Himalayan Silk Content

True silk from Uttarkashi mulberry farms is extraordinarily rare. It gives this carpet its characteristic sheen that changes with the angle of light.

Mineral Earth Dyes

The earthy tones are achieved using iron-rich clay and ochre minerals hand-ground and processed by the dye master. These pigments are more stable than plant dyes and will not fade for decades.

5 Months of Skilled Labour

Hand-tufting at this quality level requires the same skill as hand-knotting. The uniform pile and perfect texture take years of training to achieve.

GI Tag Eligible Material

The silk used is from a GI-tagged (Geographical Indication) source — a legal certification that it can only come from this specific Himalayan region.

1

Silk Reeling

Raw silk cocoons from Uttarkashi are boiled and reeled by hand onto wooden spools. A single cocoon yields up to 1,500 metres of continuous silk thread — finer than human hair.

2

Blending with Wool

The silk is hand-blended with fine Merino wool at a 40:60 ratio. This blend gives the carpet both the lustre of silk and the durability of wool — a combination rare at this price point.

3

Earth Dyeing

Clay from the Pindar riverbank is processed into a mordant-dye solution. The yarn is immersed for 48 hours, producing the signature warm earth tones that are unique to this valley's mineral composition.

4

Hand Tufting

Using a hand-held tufting gun guided by a paper stencil, the weaver punches loops of yarn through a primary backing stretched on a frame. The pattern emerges row by row, with the weaver adjusting tension by feel.

5

Latex Backing

Natural latex is applied to the back to lock every tuft permanently in place. A secondary cotton backing is then hand-stitched on. This double-backing system ensures the carpet will not shed or warp.

6

Hand Carving

The finished pile is hand-carved with scissors to create subtle texture variations and depth. This is the step that separates a master-made carpet from a machine copy — no two are identical.

The Silk Weavers of Pindar Valley

The Earth Silk Minimalist is the product of a unique collaboration between two communities: the silk farmers of Uttarkashi and the carpet weavers of the Pindar Valley in Chamoli. This partnership was established by Rug & Loom in 2019 to create a supply chain that is entirely local, traceable, and fair.

The Pindar Valley Weavers' Collective was formed in 2003 by Meena Bisht, a schoolteacher who recognised that young women in the valley were leaving for cities because traditional weaving could not sustain a livelihood. She began training a group of 8 women in modern tufting techniques while preserving traditional Garhwali patterns.

Today the collective has grown to 31 members. Their work has been featured in the Crafts Council of India's annual exhibition three times, and two of their members have received the National Award for Master Craftsperson from the Ministry of Textiles.

Caring for Silk & Wool

Vacuum with Care

Vacuum weekly along the pile direction on the lowest suction setting. Silk fibres are delicate — never pull against the pile.

Use a Rug Pad

Always place a breathable rug pad underneath. This prevents slippage, reduces wear on the backing, and allows air circulation to prevent mould.

Protect from Moisture

Silk absorbs moisture quickly. In high-humidity rooms, ensure good ventilation. If the carpet gets wet, dry flat immediately — never hang to dry as it may distort.

No Steam Cleaning

Steam will permanently damage silk fibres and loosen the latex backing. Cold water hand-cleaning only.

Keep Away from Pets

Silk fibres are particularly attractive to pets. Pet claws can snag and pull individual tufts. We recommend placing in low-traffic or pet-free areas.

Silk Specific Notes

Silk carpets have a directional sheen — they will appear lighter from one direction and darker from another. This is not a defect; it is the natural property of silk fibres refracting light. Place the carpet so the lighter direction faces the room entrance for the most flattering appearance.

Annual Care Tip

Once a year, take the carpet outside on a dry, overcast day (not direct sun) and gently beat the back with a soft broom. This removes deep-seated dust and revives the pile without any risk of damage.
